Project No. 2026-MVD-14  |  Issued for Construction  |  2026-05-15
PART 1 — GENERAL

1.1 SUMMARY

  1. Section includes molded case circuit breakers (MCCBs) for the following applications:
    1. Panelboard branch circuit breakers, 1-pole and 2-pole, 120/240V AC.
    2. Distribution feeder circuit breakers, 3-pole, 480V AC.
    3. Plug-in bus duct circuit breakers, 3-pole, 480V AC.
  2. Related Sections:
    1. Section 26 24 16 — Panelboards.
    2. Section 26 24 13 — Distribution Switchboards.

1.2 REFERENCES

  1. Comply with requirements of the following latest editions:
    1. NFPA 70 — National Electrical Code (NEC), 2020 Edition.
    2. UL 489 — Molded-Case Circuit Breakers, Molded-Case Switches, and Circuit Breaker Enclosures.
    3. NEMA AB 1 — Molded Case Circuit Breakers, Molded Case Switches, and Circuit Breaker Enclosures.
    4. NEMA AB 4 — Guidelines for Inspection and Preventive Maintenance of Molded Case Circuit Breakers Used in Commercial and Industrial Applications.

1.3 SUBMITTALS

  1. Product Data: Submit manufacturer's product data sheets for each circuit breaker type including:
    1. Voltage ratings, ampere ratings, and interrupting capacities.
    2. UL listing documentation and catalog numbers.
    3. Time-current characteristic curves for each ampere rating.
    4. Catalog number selection guide showing how options affect ratings.

1.4 QUALITY ASSURANCE

  1. All circuit breakers shall be listed and labeled by UL under UL 489.
  2. Manufacturer shall be regularly engaged in the production of circuit breakers for commercial and industrial applications with a minimum of 10 years of experience.

PART 3 — EXECUTION

3.1 INSTALLATION

  1. Install circuit breakers per manufacturer's written instructions and NEC 2020 Article 240.
  2. Tighten electrical connectors and terminals per manufacturer's published torque values using calibrated torque wrench. Record torque values on commissioning form.
  3. Do not use field-installed handle ties as a substitute for multi-pole breakers where multi-pole units are specified.
  4. Label each breaker with a laminated circuit directory per Section 26 05 53.

3.2 FIELD QUALITY CONTROL

  1. Prior to energization, verify:
    1. Catalog number matches submittal-approved product.
    2. Ampere rating and voltage rating match circuit schedule.
    3. All wiring connections are torqued to specification.
  2. Perform operational test: manually open and close each breaker three times to verify mechanical operation.
  3. Verify trip mechanism by pressing push-to-test button where provided.

3.3 CLEANING

  1. Clean interior and exterior surfaces of enclosures using clean dry cloth. Do not use solvents on circuit breaker components.
